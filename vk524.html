Musculoskeletal system injury
Musculoskeletal Injuries: Symptoms, Causes, and Treatments Musculoskeletal injuries, also known as musculoskeletal disorders, are soft tissue injuries caused by sudden impact, force, vibration and unbalanced positions. They can affect the muscles; nerves; ligaments; joints; blood vessels, neck and lower back. Types of MSD There are several musculoskeletal disorders that people suffer from, such as: Tendinitis Carpal tunnel syndrome Arthrosis Rheumatoid arthritis Fibromyalgia Bone fractures Muscle/tendon strain Ligament sprain Tension Neck Syndrome Thoracic exhaust compression Radial Tunnel Syndrome Trigger finger Degenerative disc disease Hernia and much more. Symptoms of musculoskeletal disorders People with musculoskeletal injuries can sometimes feel acute pain all over their body and muscles may twitch or burn. Swelling, numbness, and tingling can be part of MSD symptoms. The symptoms of MSD vary from person to person, but there are several symptoms common to everyone, such as: Pain Fatigue Sleep disorders Common affected areas Musculoskeletal disorders often affect the following area: Shoulders Wrists Back Hips Legs knees Feet What causes a musculoskeletal injury Most people suffer from musculoskeletal injuries due to our own lifestyle and age.
